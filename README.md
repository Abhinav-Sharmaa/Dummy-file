MCP is a universal plug for AI assistants.
Imagine your AI (like Claude) is a smart person sitting in a sealed room. They’re brilliant, but they can’t see your emails, check your calendar, or look at files on your computer. They only know what you type to them.
MCP is like cutting holes in that room and installing standard outlets. Now you can plug in things:
	•	A cable to your Google Drive
	•	A cable to your calendar
	•	A cable to your company’s database
Once plugged in, the AI can reach through and actually do stuff — read a file, send an email, look up a customer — instead of just talking about it.
Why it matters: Before MCP, every AI app had to build custom wiring for every tool. Gmail needed special code for Claude, different code for ChatGPT, different again for Gemini. MCP made one standard plug shape. Build the Gmail “adapter” once, and any AI that speaks MCP can use it.
That’s really it. The protocol, JSON-RPC, servers, clients — those are just the technical guts of how the plug works. The big idea is: one standard way to connect AI to the real world.
